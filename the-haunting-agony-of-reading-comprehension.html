The Haunting Agony of Reading Comprehension

Published on November 5, 2025, 7:23 AM | By Jersey Lois Dela Rosa

  In a classroom full of students, there shouldn't be any students who lack the ability to read; it is haunting in despair with the struggle to bestow every word in the books or on the screen. A cascade problem that has been developing throughout years, yet we are haunted by their soulless comprehension in reading amidst the day of commemoration of the souls.

  Additionally, the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) hinted at the 18.9 million functionally illiterate people across the nation in early 2025, as stated by Giselle Ombay. Furthermore, the Education Commission (EDCOM) expresses their concern over the involvement of the Department of Education (DepEd) in the existence of 24.8 million functional illiterates here in the Philippines, as for the current statistical analysis in the year 2025.

  If we hand offerings to souls, how about considering offering students a proper and progressive education system that will help them not just to read but to comprehend? We address a proper and radical solution to take action on this problem, yet it never comes into our hands.

  I recall what Rizal said in his poem, “Fair hope of my Fatherland!” If we want hope for upcoming generations, where learning and innovation can thrive, we should comprehend and urge every agency that handles the education of students to empower and inspire us with their thriving and aspiring skills as they learn to read comprehensively.

  We all urge and request a proper education system that will give hope for us, as the youth are nurtured and guided with an education system in terms of reading literacy. We're not just learning; we're shaping and building an empowering and illuminating society that will give hope and a future for us, a well-rounded society where the agencies that behold the education can strive for betterment and greater good.
